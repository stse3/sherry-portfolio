Case Study 1: Uber UX/UI Accesibility Study

What problem are we trying to solve?

Despite Uber’s 23 million daily riders and network of 5.4 million drivers worldwide, one of the growing firm’s biggest challenges is accessibility.

- Uber Global Hackathon 2023

Research: What issues will we target?

Travel Limiting Disabilities

Travel plays a key role in faciliating access to community participation and accesibility.

25.5 million Americans age 5 and older have self reported travel-limting disabilities

Wheelchair-accessible vehicles (WAV) Reliability

According to the New York Lawyers for the Public Interest (NYLPI), Uber was only able to locate a WAV 55% of the time, while non-accessible Uber vehicles were able to be located 100% of the time.

Discrimination

Targeted discrimination against individuals with disabilities or accesibility needs, including refusal to accomodate disability requests.

Our Solution: Uber Learn

Uber Learn will serve as a training platform for new Uber drivers, offering mandatory modules taken in order to complete their Uber driver registration, alongside optional modules for drivers wishing to partake in specific Uber programs such as UberPet and Uber WAV.

Mandatory Learning Modules and Programmes

Uber Learn will serve as a training platform for new Uber drivers. A score of 75% or above would be required to complete and successfully pass the module. Opportunity is given to attempt the quiz multiple times.

Physical Disabilities Awareness

Educate drivers on the limitations those with physical disabilities may experience and how to effectively aid if needed in such situations.

Invisible & Cognitive Disabilities Awareness

Accommodate for conditions like sensory or behavioral impairments by providing drivers with the toolkit to gauge the needs of their passengers without the need for intrusive questions or information

Emergency Response Module

Increasing driver and passenger safety in the vehicle by educating drivers on potential risks and emergency situations

Pets and Service Animals Module

Optional module for drivers to understand the rights of passengers with service animals and how to accommodate them safely and efficiently

Impact Projection

Barriers to Education

Uber WAV’s 15% bonus becomes a much more attractive offer if prospective drivers can sidestep the existing $100+ course for the WAV certification.

Greater Access to Accessible-Rideshare Market

Through this comparative advantage, Uber would reliably have access to the accessible-rideshare market, a substantial 27% of the American population (Centre for Disease Control, 2021).

Feature Scalability - Third Party Licensing

Uber Learn can scale to include third-party licensing programs once the initial phase has been refined. By partnering with additional service and hospitality providers, Uber Learn has immense growth potential.
