Case Study 1: Uber UX/UI Accesibility Study

What problem are we trying to solve?

Despite Uber’s 23 million daily riders and network of 5.4 million drivers worldwide, one of the growing firm’s biggest challenges is accessibility.

Research: What issues will we target?

Travel Limiting Disabilities

Travel plays a key role in faciliating access to community participation and accesibility.

25.5 million Americans age 5 and older have self reported travel-limting disabilities

Wheelchair-accessible vehicles (WAV) Reliability

According to the New York Lawyers for the Public Interest (NYLPI), Uber was only able to locat a WAV 55% of the time, while non-accessible Uber vehicles were able to be located 100% of the time.

Discrimination

Targeted discrimnation against individuals with disabilities or accesibility needs, including refusal of accomdoating requests.

Our Solution: Uber Learn

Uber Learn will serve as a training platform for new Uber drivers, offering mandatory modules taken in order to complete their Uber driver registration, alongside optional modules for drivers wishing to partake in specific Uber programs such as UberPet and Uber WAV.
