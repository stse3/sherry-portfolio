Case Study 1: Uber UX/UI Accesibility Study

What problem are we trying to solve?

Despite Uber’s 23 million daily riders and network of 5.4 million drivers worldwide, one of the growing firm’s biggest challenges is accessibility.

Research: What issues will we target?

Our Solution: Uber Learn

Uber Learn will serve as a training platform for new Uber drivers, offering mandatory modules taken in order to complete their Uber driver registration, alongside optional modules for drivers wishing to partake in specific Uber programs such as UberPet and Uber WAV

Impact Projection

Uber Learn can be expanded to cater towards all Uber services and specific niches—for example, relevant education or training for Uber Eats or Uber Health drivers. Through Uber Learn’s new service, we hope to improve the experience of both drivers and riders alike. Creating a program for drivers can also substantially improve the satisfaction of our riders. Not only that, training removes the barrier of trustability and assures new riders that they will be in good hands. Such additional certifications can be denoted by Uber’s existing driver profiles, or alternate icons within the app to inform riders of the driver’s training

Through this comparative advantage, Uber would reliably have access to the accessible-rideshare market, a substantial 27% of the American population (Centre for Disease Control, 2021).
